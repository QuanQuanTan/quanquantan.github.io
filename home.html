About me

I am currently working as a cybersecurity engineer at the Centre for Strategic Infocomm Technologies (CSIT), Singapore.

In 2023, I obtained my Ph.D. from the Division of Mathematical Sciences at Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore, under the supervision of Thomas Peyrin. My Ph.D. thesis, titled "Cryptanalysis of Lightweight Symmetric-Key Cryptographic Algorithms," focused on developing and automating tools to assist in the cryptanalysis of symmetric-key ciphers. Prior to this, I earned my B.Sc. in Mathematical Sciences with a specialization in Applied Mathematics, as well as an M.Sc. (Research) in Mathematical Sciences from NTU. My master's research centered on optimization techniques in the implementation of block ciphers. My research interests include developing automated tools for cryptanalysis and designing secure symmetric key primitives. More generally, I am keen to explore anything related to cryptography.
